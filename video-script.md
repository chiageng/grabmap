## Section 3 — AI SCOUT DEMO (0:30 – 1:30)

**Visual — 0:30:** Click the green **⚡ Ask AI** button. Modal opens.

**Voiceover:**

> Let's roleplay. I'm a hawker thinking about Lavender MRT. I'll type what I want in plain English.

*"I want to build a chicken rice shop near Lavender MRT, help me analyse the competitors"*

Click **Analyze**.

**Voiceover (while loading, ~3 seconds):**

> Behind the scenes — Claude parses my sentence into a business type and seven category keywords. GrabMaps geocodes Lavender MRT. We fire eight parallel searches against their POI network. Three seconds later…

**Voiceover (point cursor at the Recommendation Score card):**

> …the full analysis. Recommendation Score: **fifty-eight out of one hundred**. Verdict: **Workable but competitive**. And the breakdown is transparent — competition zero, because forty-seven direct competitors is oversaturated — but accessibility perfect one hundred, because we're literally at the MRT exit.

**Visual — 1:05:** Scroll down inside the report panel to the **Competitor Radar** section. Hover over rank #1.

**Voiceover:**

> Here's the engineering detail. Competitors are ranked by **relevance**, not distance. *Wow Chicken Rice* at fifty-three meters is rank one. *McDonald's* — physically the closest shop — is rank seven, because name-matching "chicken rice" beats raw proximity. No other scouting tool does this.

**Visual — 1:20:** Pan cursor to the red heatmap on the map, circle a couple of cold spots.

**Voiceover:**

> The red heatmap shows exactly where competitors cluster. The cold zones are gaps in coverage. Any SME owner can read this map — no analyst required.

## Recording tips

### Pacing
- Average reading speed **160 words per minute**. Count your words per section and time yourself.
- **Slow down on key numbers** — "fifty-eight out of one hundred", "fifteen-point swing", "two hundred thousand dollars"
- **Pause after hook lines** — let *"closes that gap"* and *"for the ninety-nine percent"* breathe for half a beat

### Voiceover tone
- Confident, not salesy
- Emphasize verbs: *"closes"*, *"rank one"*, *"zoom"*, *"opens"*
- Flatten tone slightly on technical bits (Claude parses, GrabMaps geocodes) — it's context, not drama

### Visual cadence
- Cursor should lead the eye, not follow the voice
- Let visuals land 0.5–1s before speaking about them
- When both panels appear in compare mode, give **2 silent seconds** before speaking — judges need to see it
- Use smooth cursor movements, no jitter. Consider `Smoothcam` or macOS's built-in cursor smoothing

### Editing after recording
- Cut any "ums" / restarts ruthlessly — 3 minutes is tight
- Add 300ms of **ambient crossfade** between sections so it doesn't feel choppy
- Boom the Grab click with a **subtle whoosh or pop SFX** to emphasize the integration moment
- Bake in a bottom-corner watermark: `grabmap.vercel.app` · Smarter Places · [your team name]
- Closing frame can hold for an extra 1–2s on the URL before fade to black

### Background music (optional but recommended)
- Soft instrumental, no vocals, 60–80 BPM
- Examples: lofi beats, minimal piano, soft electronic
- Duck to -18dB under voice; swell to -10dB during the hero moment (2-second silence when compare appears)

## Fallbacks if something goes wrong mid-recording

- **If the scout takes longer than 4 seconds**: extend the "behind the scenes" voiceover with one more sentence: *"...eight parallel GrabMaps requests, one Claude call, all fanned out and merged..."*
- **If the Bugis result looks underwhelming** (e.g. similar score): swap the compare target to **Tampines Mall** or **Chinatown** during practice, whichever gives the most contrast with Lavender
- **If the red/blue heatmaps don't render vividly**: zoom the map out slightly so both heatmap centers are visible; MapLibre's heatmap radius scales with zoom
- **If the Grab deep link doesn't do anything on desktop during recording**: say *"on mobile this opens the Grab app directly — here's a screenshot of that flow"* and cut to a pre-captured phone screenshot

## One last thing before you hit record

Read the whole script aloud **three times**. The first read will be stilted. The second will be better. **The third take is the one you record.** Confidence comes from repetition.
